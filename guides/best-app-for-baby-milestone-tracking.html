Key Developmental Milestones by Age

3 months: Smiling socially, tracking objects with eyes, lifting head briefly during tummy time.

6 months: Rolling both ways, babbling, reaching for objects, sitting with support.

12 months: Cruising furniture, saying 2-3 words, pointing, showing emotions.

18 months: Walking independently, 10+ word vocabulary, following simple directions, showing empathy.

24 months: Running, 50+ word vocabulary, combining words, engaging in pretend play.

3 years: Pedaling tricycle, 250+ word vocabulary, telling stories, forming friendships.

When to Seek Developmental Screening

Consult your pediatrician if baby: isn't reaching multiple milestones by recommended age, loses skills previously acquired, has limited communication or social interaction, shows repetitive movements or behaviors, has vision or hearing concerns, or you have gut feeling something is different.

Important: Wide variation is normal. One baby walks at 9 months, another at 16 months—both are typically developing. Developmental screening looks at overall pattern, not single milestones.

How to Use Milestone Tracking Effectively

Don't obsess: Milestones are ranges, not deadlines. Comparing to other babies creates unnecessary anxiety.

Document with photos/videos: Visual records help you remember early development and can support provider conversations about concerns.

Use for communication: Bring milestone data to well-visits. It helps pediatricians assess development and provides baseline for screening if concerns arise.

Track patterns, not dates: A baby reaching 3 milestones in one month, then nothing for 2 months, matters more than missing one specific milestone on schedule.
