Expert Insight: When to Start Feeding Tracking

"I recommend parents start tracking feeding around day 5 after birth. For breastfeeding families, tracking helps us spot latch issues and oversupply/undersupply early. For formula families, it confirms adequate intake. By 2-3 months, patterns are clear and you can often stop formal tracking."

— Dr. Lisa Park, Lactation Consultant & Pediatrician

How to Track Different Feeding Types

Breastfeeding: Log duration on each side, latch quality concerns, and any pain. Track wet diapers (should increase by day 5-6). Most apps let you note supply observations.

Bottle Feeding: Log amount consumed (for formula or pumped milk), type of formula, and preparation details. Easier to quantify than breastfeeding, so apps can provide clearer "is baby eating enough?" insights.

Mixed Feeding: Track breastfeeding and bottle feeds separately. This helps you optimize the mix if you're gradually weaning or exclusively pumping.

Solids Introduction (6+ months): Log foods tried, amounts, reactions, and any concerns. Best apps flag common allergens and provide introduction guidance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should a newborn eat? Can an app tell me if my baby is eating enough?
Newborns typically feed 8-12 times per 24 hours, with intake increasing from 10ml on day 1 to 60ml+ by day 5. Quality apps (especially bottle-tracking ones) can tell you total daily intake and flag concerns. For breastfeeding, wet diaper count is more reliable than app predictions.
When should I introduce solids? Can an app help?
Solids are recommended around 6 months when baby shows readiness signs (sitting upright, loss of tongue-thrust reflex, interest in food). Apps like Wermom provide introduction schedules and allergen guidelines, but pediatrician approval is essential.
Is there a "best" feeding app for exclusively pumping parents?
Yes. Apps with strong bottle-feeding tracking and supply/demand notes work best. Wermom and BabyCenter both handle pumping well, though Wermom has more optimization features.
Should I log every feeding or just when concerned?
For the first 4-6 weeks, frequent logging (especially for breastfeeding) helps identify patterns and problems. After that, 4-5 days per week is enough to spot trends without fatigue.
Can a nutrition-tracking app replace pediatric guidance on feeding?
No. Apps provide insights and tracking, but pediatricians assess actual growth, development, and nutritional status. Use apps as a communication tool—bring data to appointments.
