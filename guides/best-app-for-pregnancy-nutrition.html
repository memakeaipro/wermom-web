Pregnancy Nutrition by Trimester

First Trimester: No additional calories needed (despite nausea and food aversions). Focus on prenatal vitamins, especially folate (400-800 mcg). Aim for varied diet: protein, whole grains, fruits, vegetables, dairy. Manage nausea with small, frequent meals and ginger.

Second Trimester: Add 300-350 calories per day (equivalent to one snack). Focus on iron-rich foods (anemia common). Calcium critical for bone development (1000 mg/day). DHA important for baby brain development (200-300 mg/day).

Third Trimester: Continue 300-350 additional calories. Protein needs increase (60-70 grams/day). Continue iron and calcium. Watch weight gain (healthy range 25-35 lbs depending on pre-pregnancy weight).

Key Nutrients During Pregnancy

Folate (400-800 mcg): Prevents neural tube defects. Found in leafy greens, legumes, fortified grains. Prenatal vitamin is standard supplement.

Iron (27 mg): Increased need for blood volume expansion. Iron-rich: lean meat, poultry, legumes, fortified cereals. Pair with vitamin C for absorption. Constipation and nausea are common side effects of supplements.

Calcium (1000 mg): Baby's bone and teeth development. Dairy, fortified plant-based milks, leafy greens. Important: if inadequate, body pulls calcium from your bones.

DHA (200-300 mg): Omega-3 fatty acid crucial for baby's brain and eye development. Fatty fish (low-mercury varieties), fish oil supplements, or fortified foods.

Protein (60-70 grams): Critical for placenta, amniotic fluid, and fetal development. Meat, poultry, fish, eggs, legumes, dairy, nuts.

Foods to Avoid During Pregnancy

High-mercury fish: Shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish. Limit to 6 oz per week: canned tuna, pollock. Safe: salmon, shrimp, light tuna (up to 12 oz/week).

Unpasteurized products: Unpasteurized milk, soft cheeses (brie, feta, queso fresco), raw/undercooked deli meats, raw sprouts, undercooked eggs/meat (risk of listeria, salmonella).

Alcohol and caffeine: Alcohol: no safe amount known (fetal alcohol spectrum disorder risk). Caffeine: limit to 200 mg/day (equivalent to one 12oz cup of coffee).

Raw/undercooked: Sushi with raw fish, undercooked meat/poultry, unwashed produce.

FAQ: Pregnancy Nutrition

Q: Do I really need to eat for two?
A: No. You need only 300-350 additional calories per day (from second trimester). That's equivalent to one snack. Eating too much can lead to excessive weight gain and pregnancy complications.

Q: What if I can't take prenatal vitamins?
A: Discuss with your OB. Some people have nausea with prenatal vitamins. Alternatives: taking at different time of day, gummy prenatals, or divided doses.

Q: Should I be eating organic during pregnancy?
A: Conventional produce is safe if washed. If budget allows, prioritize organic for "Dirty Dozen" (highest pesticide residues). Most important: eat plenty of fruits and vegetables in any form.

Q: Can I diet during pregnancy?
A: No intentional weight loss. However, if you're overweight, weight gain at lower end of range (11-20 lbs) under medical supervision is acceptable. Never restrict calories during pregnancy.
