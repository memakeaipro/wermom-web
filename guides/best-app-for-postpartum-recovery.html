Expert Perspective on Postpartum Recovery

"Postpartum recovery is 6-8 weeks minimum for vaginal delivery, 8-12 weeks for cesarean. Apps that normalize this timeline and track milestones help mothers understand their recovery isn't linear. Some days are harder than others, and that's normal. The best apps flag actual complications early."

— Dr. Jennifer Torres, OB/Postpartum Wellness Specialist

Common Postpartum Concerns to Track

Bleeding: Expect heavy bleeding (soaking pads) for 1-2 weeks, then gradual decrease. If bleeding increases after day 7-10, contact provider immediately.

Pain: Normal: soreness at perineal tears/episiotomy or incision. Not normal: severe pain, increasing pain, pus, fever (signs of infection).

Mood: Baby blues (crying, mood swings) in first 2 weeks are normal. Postpartum depression (persistent sadness, disconnection, intrusive thoughts) requires provider intervention. Track mood daily to catch concerning patterns.

Breastfeeding: Nipple pain is common first 1-2 weeks. Persistent severe pain, bleeding, or signs of mastitis (fever, red, swollen breast) need attention.

When to Contact Your Provider

Seek immediate care if you experience: heavy bleeding (soaking more than one pad/hour), signs of infection (fever, foul discharge, increasing pain), chest pain, shortness of breath, severe headache, vision changes, thoughts of harming yourself or baby, inability to care for yourself or baby.

Schedule appointment if: Pain not improving by week 3-4, persistent constipation after bowel movements, urinary issues (incontinence beyond 6 weeks, inability to empty bladder), depression/anxiety symptoms lasting beyond 2 weeks.
