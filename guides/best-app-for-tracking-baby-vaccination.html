Understanding Vaccine Safety

Common side effects (normal): Redness/soreness at injection site, low fever (especially rotavirus), fussiness, mild rash (especially after MMR). Typically resolve within 48 hours.

When to contact provider: High fever (>104°F), seizure, severe allergic reaction, unusual crying that lasts hours. These are rare but warrant immediate medical attention.

Vaccine ingredients transparency: All vaccines undergo rigorous safety testing before and after approval. Ingredients serve specific purposes (preservatives prevent contamination, adjuvants strengthen immune response). No evidence links vaccines to autism (the original fraudulent study has been retracted and author lost medical license).

Important: Questions about vaccines? Discuss with your pediatrician. They can address specific concerns about your baby's health or family history.

Alternative Vaccination Schedules

Some parents choose delayed or alternative vaccination schedules. This is a personal choice to discuss with your pediatrician. Important: delaying vaccines leaves babies unprotected during vulnerable periods. If you choose an alternative schedule, work with a pediatrician—they can help you protect your baby while respecting your preferences.

Note: Most daycare and schools require vaccinations per CDC schedule or proof of exemption. Vaccination records are essential for enrollment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get vaccination records from the app?
A: Yes. Wermom stores vaccination records securely and generates documents for daycare/school documentation.

Q: What if my baby has a medical condition? Should I still vaccinate?
A: Most medical conditions don't contraindicate vaccination. Some (like severe immunocompromise) require modified schedules. Discuss with your pediatrician.

Q: What's the difference between Tdap and DTaP?
A: DTaP is for babies/children (contains diphtheria, tetanus, acellular pertussis). Tdap is the booster for adolescents/adults (lower diphtheria dose). Both protect against the same diseases.

Q: Is it ever too late to catch up on vaccines?
A: No. Catch-up schedules exist for any age. If your baby missed doses, talk to your pediatrician about getting back on track.
