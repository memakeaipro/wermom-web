Launch Day Strategy & Tips

Timeline

12:00 AM (Midnight before launch)
Post across social media announcing it's almost live. Build hype.
6:00 AM Launch Time
Your product goes live on Product Hunt. Immediately post the maker comment with your offer.
6:15 AM
Post across all social channels: Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram. Share the Product Hunt link.
6:30 AM - 9:00 AM
Monitor comments and reply to EVERY comment within minutes. Early momentum is critical.
9:00 AM - 12:00 PM
Post follow-up tweets. Engage with people who are commenting. Ask questions, respond to feedback.
12:00 PM
Post on LinkedIn with founder angle. Consider a blog post on Medium explaining what you built and why.
12:00 PM - 6:00 PM
Peak engagement hours. Respond to every comment. Share user stories if you have any. Post follow-up tweets.
6:00 PM - 12:00 AM
Continue engagement but slower pace. Prepare for next day (you need to stay engaged for 48 hours).
Day 2 & 3
Stay engaged, reply to comments, thank people for feedback, share wins and testimonials.

Critical Success Factors

  • Reply to EVERY comment in the first 6 hours. This is how you rank higher on Product Hunt. Speed matters.
  • Be authentic. Share your story. Talk about why you built this. Users connect with founders.
  • Ask for feedback. "What features would make this better for you?" People will respond and you'll get insights.
  • Share testimonials. If you have users already, ask them to leave reviews. Real stories convert.
  • Be present for 48 hours. This is a marathon, not a sprint. You need engagement through day 2 to keep momentum.
  • Don't oversell. Product Hunt users can smell inauthenticity. Be honest about what you've built and what you're working on.

If You Hit #1

  • Post about it across social media. This is worth celebrating.
  • Screenshot the #1 badge. Use it in future marketing.
  • Thank the community. You got here because people believed in what you built.
  • Offer bonus perks if you haven't already (extended trial, discount, etc.)

If You Don't Hit #1

  • You still won. You launched in public and got feedback. That's a win.
  • Collect feedback and keep improving.
  • Stay engaged with the Product Hunt community.
  • Plan for a future launch when you have more to share.
Pro tip: Product Hunt ranking is partly based on upvotes, partly on engagement. The more you engage, the higher you'll rank. Be present.

Anticipated Q&A for Launch Day

Q: How is this different from [COMPETITOR]?

A: [COMPETITOR] focuses on [feature/angle]. We focus on helping parents understand their baby's health through trend visualization and pattern recognition. We're not trying to be everything—we're trying to be really good at one thing: making health data useful.

Q: Is this HIPAA compliant?

A: We follow healthcare security best practices. All data is encrypted. We don't sell your data to third parties. We're working on HIPAA compliance certification and that's coming [DATE].

Q: What if I'm not tech-savvy?

A: We designed this to be simple. Logging takes 30 seconds. No complicated navigation. If you can use text messages and take photos, you can use Wermom. We have customer support if you need help.

Q: Why should I pay for this when there are free apps?

A: Free apps make money by collecting and selling your data or showing you ads. Wermom is paid because we believe your baby's health data should be private. You're not the product, you're the customer. Plus, most free apps give you a log. We give you insights.

Q: What's your roadmap?

A: We're currently focused on [FEATURE]. Next quarter we're building [FEATURE]. Long-term, we'd love to integrate with EHR systems and partner with pediatric clinics. We're taking feedback from users to shape our roadmap.

Pro tip: Have these answers ready before launch. First commenters will ask these questions. Quick, thoughtful responses build credibility.

Post-Launch (Days 2-7)

Keep the momentum going:

  • Send thank you emails to everyone who upvoted or commented
  • Share wins: "We had 500 signups on day 1!" (if true)
  • Post user testimonials on social media
  • Collect feedback and publicly commit to improvements
  • Reach out to media with the story: "Product launched and immediately got X upvotes"
  • Cross-post key discussions to Medium, LinkedIn
  • Engage with parenting communities on Reddit and forums
  • Send follow-up emails to free trial users encouraging them to actually use the product
Pro tip: Product Hunt momentum doesn't end at 48 hours. You can still get press coverage, user signups, and testimonials for weeks after launch. Stay engaged with the community.
