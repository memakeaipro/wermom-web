20 Questions Every New Parent Should Ask Their Pediatrician

Parenting GuideBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: Don't leave your pediatrician appointments wishing you'd asked something. Here are the most important questions for each stage, organized by visit type.

The Complete List

  1. 1. What's my baby's growth percentile trend — is the trajectory healthy? (Not the number, but the direction)
  2. 2. Is my baby getting enough to eat? (Discuss feeding amounts, weight gain rate, and diaper output) —
  3. 3. What are the next developmental milestones to watch for? (Get age-specific expectations) —
  4. 4. Is my baby's sleep amount normal for their age? (Discuss both total and nighttime sleep) —
  5. 5. When should I worry about this symptom? (Be specific about what you're seeing) —
  6. 6. What are the expected side effects of today's vaccines? (Know what's normal vs concerning) —
  7. 7. What medications are safe at this age? (Dosing by weight for fever reducers, etc.) —
  8. 8. When should I introduce solid foods, and what first? (Personalized to your baby's readiness) —
  9. 9. How do I know if my baby has allergies? (Signs, testing, and when to introduce common allergens) —
  10. 10. Is my baby's poop normal? (Color, consistency, and frequency by age) —
  11. 11. When should I be concerned about a fever? (Temperature thresholds by age) —
  12. 12. What's your after-hours policy? (Know how to reach someone at 2 AM) —
  13. 13. How do I babyproof for my baby's current developmental stage? (Updates as baby becomes mobile) —
  14. 14. Are there any concerns about my baby's hearing or vision? (Screening schedules and signs) —
  15. 15. What should I know about upcoming sleep changes? (Regressions, nap transitions, etc.) —
  16. 16. Is screen time OK at this age? (Get an honest, practical answer) —
  17. 17. How can I support my baby's language development? (Practical daily strategies) —
  18. 18. Are there any vitamin or mineral supplements my baby needs? (Vitamin D, iron, etc.) —
  19. 19. What are the red flags I should watch for before the next visit? (Age-specific warning signs) —
  20. 20. Is there anything in the Wermom health data I should be concerned about? (Bring tracking data to appointments) —

Why This Matters

These aren't just random tips — they're drawn from pediatric research, nurse expertise, and thousands of real parent experiences. Each item addresses a specific need that new parents commonly face. Bookmark this list and come back to it as your baby grows.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are these tips evidence-based?

Yes, all items in this list are backed by current pediatric guidelines, medical research, or expert recommendations from healthcare professionals. We review and update our content regularly to reflect the latest evidence.

What if something on this list doesn't work for my baby?

Every baby is unique. Use this list as a starting point, but trust your parental instincts and your baby's individual cues. If a strategy isn't working after a fair trial, try an alternative approach or consult your pediatrician for personalized advice.
