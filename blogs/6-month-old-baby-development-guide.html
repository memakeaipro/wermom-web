6-Month-Old Baby: Development, Milestones & Care Tips

Quick Answer: At 6 months, babies typically show specific developmental milestones in motor skills, cognitive abilities, and social-emotional growth. Each baby develops at their own pace.

Key Milestones at 6 Months

By 6 months, most babies achieve important developmental milestones. These include advances in motor skills like rolling, sitting, or walking depending on age, as well as cognitive developments like object permanence and social smiling.

Feeding Your 6-Month-Old

At 6 months, your baby's nutritional needs are evolving. Whether you're breastfeeding, formula feeding, or introducing solids, it's important to follow age-appropriate feeding guidelines and watch for hunger and fullness cues.

Sleep Schedule at 6 Months

Your 6-month-old's sleep patterns are developing. Understanding age-appropriate sleep needs, establishing healthy bedtime routines, and managing nap transitions can help everyone in the family get better rest.
