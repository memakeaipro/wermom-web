Pregnancy Week 13: Symptoms, Baby Size & What to Expect

PregnancyBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: At week 13 of pregnancy, your baby is about the size of a lemon and weighs approximately ~23g. Here's everything happening this week — from your baby's development to the symptoms you might experience, plus expert tips to help you through.

Your Baby at Week 13

Your baby measures roughly the size of a lemon (2.9 in) and weighs about ~23g. The major developments this week: Entering 2nd trimester — vocal cords developing, intestines moving from umbilical cord into abdomen, fingerprints complete.

Your baby's vocal cords are forming this week — the same ones that will produce that first cry at birth.

Symptoms at Week 13

Common symptoms during week 13 of pregnancy include: Energy returning as 2nd trimester begins, appetite improving, libido may increase, round ligament pain, less nausea.

Remember that every pregnancy is unique — you may experience all, some, or none of these symptoms. If anything feels unusual or concerning, don't hesitate to contact your healthcare provider.

What to Do This Week

Welcome to the second trimester! Many women call this the 'honeymoon phase' of pregnancy. Energy levels typically improve significantly. This is a great time to start gentle exercise if you haven't already.

Nutrition & Wellness

Staying well-nourished is crucial at every stage of pregnancy. Focus on folate-rich foods (leafy greens, lentils, fortified cereals), iron (lean meats, beans, spinach), calcium (dairy, fortified plant milks), and omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, walnuts, flaxseed). Stay hydrated with at least 8-10 glasses of water daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to feel [symptom] at week 13?

Yes, most symptoms at week 13 are completely normal. Common experiences include Energy returning as 2nd trimester begins and appetite improving. However, contact your doctor if you experience severe pain, heavy bleeding, or persistent vomiting that prevents you from keeping fluids down.

How big is my baby at 13 weeks?

At week 13, your baby is approximately the size of a lemon, weighing about ~23g. Entering 2nd trimester — vocal cords developing.

What should I avoid at week 13 of pregnancy?

Continue avoiding alcohol, raw/undercooked meats and fish, unpasteurized dairy, high-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel), deli meats unless heated until steaming, and limit caffeine to under 200mg per day.
