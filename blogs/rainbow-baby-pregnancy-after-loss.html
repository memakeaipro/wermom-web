Rainbow Baby: Navigating Pregnancy After Loss

LifestyleBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: A rainbow baby — a child born after a pregnancy loss — brings both profound joy and complex emotions. It's normal to feel excited, scared, guilty, and hopeful all at once.

The Emotional Complexity

Pregnancy after loss is not a simple 'happy ending.' Many parents experience: anxiety that intensifies rather than decreases as pregnancy progresses, difficulty bonding with the new pregnancy, guilt (feeling disloyal to the baby they lost), hypervigilance about every symptom, grief for the lost pregnancy coexisting with joy about the new one. All of these feelings are normal and valid.

Medical Considerations

Depending on the type of loss, your provider may recommend: earlier and more frequent prenatal visits, additional monitoring (more ultrasounds, non-stress tests), progesterone supplementation for some recurrent losses, cervical length monitoring after second-trimester loss, and a plan for delivery timing. Ask about the specific cause of your previous loss and what, if anything, is different this time.

Coping Strategies

Take it one day at a time — milestone by milestone instead of counting down months. It's OK to not share the pregnancy publicly until you're ready. Continue to honor the baby you lost — this pregnancy doesn't replace that loss. Find a therapist who specializes in pregnancy after loss. Connect with other rainbow baby parents (online communities can be invaluable). Give yourself permission to feel whatever you feel.
