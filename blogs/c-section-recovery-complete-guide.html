The First 24 Hours Post-Surgery

Many parents find themselves searching for answers about the first 24 hours post-surgery. The key concepts include: Pain management, mobility goals, catheter removal, first breastfeed positioning, emotional processing.

At the core of this topic is pain management. Medical professionals consistently emphasize this as a starting point for informed parenting. Understanding these basics helps you have more productive conversations with your pediatrician and make confident decisions about your child's care.

Another crucial factor involves mobility goals. This works in tandem with catheter removal to give parents the full picture. Many experienced pediatricians note that parents who understand both of these concepts tend to identify potential issues earlier.

What does this look like day-to-day? For most families, it means being intentional about monitoring c-section recovery and noting any changes from what's typical for your child. You don't need to be obsessive about it — just consistent. A few quick notes each day can paint a powerful picture over time.
