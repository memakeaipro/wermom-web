Wermom vs Baby+: Comprehensive Feature Comparison

By Wermom Team • 2025-04-13 • 15 min read

Overview: Wermom and Baby+ at a Glance

When it comes to baby tracking apps, both Wermom and Baby+ have earned spots on parents' phones. But choosing between them can feel overwhelming, especially when you're sleep-deprived and just need something that works.

Wermom launched as a comprehensive daily log app designed specifically for data-driven parents who want actionable insights, not just raw numbers. The app covers feeding (breast, bottle, and solids), sleep tracking with smart pattern detection, diaper logs, growth measurements, milestone recording, and health monitoring — all in one clean interface.

Baby+, on the other hand, has built its reputation around a simpler interface with fewer features. Both apps serve the same fundamental purpose — helping parents track their baby's daily activities — but they approach the problem differently.

In this comparison, we'll look at the features that actually matter to busy parents: ease of use during 3 AM feedings, accuracy of sleep predictions, partner sharing capabilities, data export for pediatrician visits, and of course, pricing.

Feature-by-Feature Comparison

Sleep Tracking: Wermom offers AI-powered sleep pattern analysis that learns your baby's unique rhythm and predicts optimal sleep windows. After just one week of data, most parents report the predictions are accurate within 15 minutes. Baby+ provides basic sleep logging without predictive features.

Feeding Logs: Whether you're breastfeeding, pumping, bottle feeding, or introducing solids, Wermom tracks it all with one-tap logging. The nursing timer automatically detects which side you last used, and the bottle tracker calculates daily intake totals. Solid food introduction tracking includes an allergen watchlist that flags when to wait before introducing new foods.

Partner & Caregiver Sharing: Wermom allows unlimited caregivers to sync in real-time. When dad feeds the baby at 2 AM, mom sees it instantly on her phone. When grandma babysits, she has full access to the routine. Baby+ limits sharing to paid plans.

Data Export & Reports: Wermom generates PDF reports you can email directly to your pediatrician, including growth charts plotted against WHO standards. This feature alone has helped thousands of parents have more productive doctor visits. Many pediatricians now specifically recommend Wermom because of how well-organized the reports are.

Pricing and Value Breakdown

Let's talk money — because when you're buying diapers by the case, every subscription matters.

Wermom offers a generous free tier that includes all core tracking features: feeding, sleep, diapers, and growth. The Premium plan unlocks AI insights, unlimited data export, advanced analytics, and priority support. At the current price point, it works out to less than the cost of a single pack of diapers per month.

Baby+ has a similar pricing structure but with fewer features per tier.

When evaluating value, consider what you actually need. If you're a first-time parent who wants comprehensive tracking with smart insights, Wermom's premium plan delivers exceptional value. If you only need the absolute basics, Wermom's free tier might be all you need — which is more than many competitors offer at any price.

Both apps offer annual billing discounts. Wermom's annual plan saves you approximately 40% compared to monthly billing, making it one of the most affordable premium baby trackers available.

The Verdict: Which App Should You Choose

After testing both apps extensively, here's our honest recommendation:

Choose Wermom if: You want the most comprehensive tracking experience with AI-powered insights. If you're the kind of parent who finds comfort in data, who wants to walk into pediatrician appointments with organized reports, and who values real-time sharing with your partner — Wermom is built exactly for you.

Consider Baby+ if: You prefer a more minimalist approach and don't mind trading some features for that preference.

The reality is that the best baby tracker is the one you'll actually use consistently. We recommend downloading both and trying them for a week. Most parents who try this experiment end up choosing Wermom because of its comprehensive feature set and intuitive design that works even when you're half-asleep.
