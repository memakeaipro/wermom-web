Can You Eat Sushi While Pregnant? Complete Safety Guide

Health & Parenting FAQBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: Raw fish sushi should be avoided during pregnancy due to the risk of parasites and bacteria. However, many cooked sushi options and vegetable rolls are perfectly safe — you don't have to give up sushi entirely for 9 months.

Why Raw Fish Is Risky

Raw fish can harbor parasites (like Anisakis), bacteria (like Listeria and Salmonella), and may contain higher levels of mercury. During pregnancy, your immune system is naturally suppressed, making you more susceptible to foodborne illnesses. Listeria infection during pregnancy can cause miscarriage, stillbirth, or serious neonatal infection.

Safe Sushi Options During Pregnancy

You can still enjoy sushi with fully cooked ingredients: shrimp tempura rolls, California rolls (imitation crab is cooked), eel (unagi) rolls (fully cooked), cooked salmon or tuna rolls, vegetable rolls (cucumber, avocado, sweet potato), and edamame. Ensure rice is freshly prepared and served at proper temperature.

Mercury Concerns in Fish

Even for cooked fish, choose low-mercury options: salmon, shrimp, pollock, tilapia, catfish, and cod are good choices. Limit albacore tuna to 6 oz per week. Avoid high-mercury fish entirely: shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish, bigeye tuna, and marlin. The FDA recommends 2-3 servings of low-mercury fish per week during pregnancy for omega-3 benefits.

What About Sushi from High-End Restaurants?

Even at the finest restaurants with sashimi-grade fish, the risk isn't zero. Flash-freezing (required in the US for fish served raw) kills parasites but doesn't eliminate all bacteria. The safest approach during pregnancy is to stick with fully cooked sushi options — it's only 9 months, and the alternatives are delicious.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is imitation crab in California rolls safe during pregnancy?

Yes! Imitation crab (surimi) is made from cooked pollock and is safe during pregnancy. California rolls with imitation crab, avocado, and cucumber are a great pregnancy-safe sushi option. Check that the restaurant uses imitation crab and not real crab (which should be cooked).

Can I eat smoked salmon on sushi?

Cold-smoked salmon (lox) carries a Listeria risk and should be avoided during pregnancy. Hot-smoked salmon that's been fully cooked to 145°F is safe. If you're unsure how the salmon was prepared, ask the restaurant or choose a fully cooked option instead.

I accidentally ate raw sushi while pregnant — should I worry?

Don't panic. The actual risk from a single exposure is low. Monitor for symptoms of foodborne illness over the next few days: fever, nausea, diarrhea, or muscle aches. If you develop any symptoms, contact your OB-GYN promptly and mention the raw fish exposure.
