Pregnancy Week 9: Symptoms, Baby Size & What to Expect

PregnancyBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: At week 9 of pregnancy, your baby is about the size of a cherry and weighs approximately ~2g. Here's everything happening this week — from your baby's development to the symptoms you might experience, plus expert tips to help you through.

Your Baby at Week 9

Your baby measures roughly the size of a cherry and weighs about ~2g. The major developments this week: All essential organs have formed (organogenesis complete), tiny fingers visible, can make movements (too small to feel).

Your baby's fingerprints are already beginning to form at just 9 weeks — unique patterns that will last a lifetime.

Symptoms at Week 9

Common symptoms during week 9 of pregnancy include: Morning sickness may be peaking, mood swings, round ligament pain beginning, nasal congestion, visible veins on breasts.

Remember that every pregnancy is unique — you may experience all, some, or none of these symptoms. If anything feels unusual or concerning, don't hesitate to contact your healthcare provider.

What to Do This Week

If morning sickness is severe (can't keep liquids down, losing weight), talk to your doctor — you may have hyperemesis gravidarum which needs treatment.

Nutrition & Wellness

Staying well-nourished is crucial at every stage of pregnancy. Focus on folate-rich foods (leafy greens, lentils, fortified cereals), iron (lean meats, beans, spinach), calcium (dairy, fortified plant milks), and omega-3 fatty acids (salmon, walnuts, flaxseed). Stay hydrated with at least 8-10 glasses of water daily.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it normal to feel [symptom] at week 9?

Yes, most symptoms at week 9 are completely normal. Common experiences include Morning sickness may be peaking and mood swings. However, contact your doctor if you experience severe pain, heavy bleeding, or persistent vomiting that prevents you from keeping fluids down.

How big is my baby at 9 weeks?

At week 9, your baby is approximately the size of a cherry, weighing about ~2g. All essential organs have formed (organogenesis complete).

What should I avoid at week 9 of pregnancy?

Continue avoiding alcohol, raw/undercooked meats and fish, unpasteurized dairy, high-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel), deli meats unless heated until steaming, and limit caffeine to under 200mg per day.
