Twin Pregnancy Guide: What's Different & How to Prepare

LifestyleBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: Twin pregnancies are classified as high-risk but result in healthy babies the vast majority of the time. Understanding the differences from singleton pregnancies helps you prepare and know what to expect.

Types of Twins

Identical (monozygotic): One egg splits into two embryos. These twins share DNA and may share a placenta (monochorionic) or have separate placentas (dichorionic). Monochorionic twins require closer monitoring for twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome (TTTS). Fraternal (dizygotic): Two separate eggs fertilized by two sperm. Each has their own placenta. They're as genetically similar as any siblings.

What's Different About Twin Pregnancy

More prenatal visits and ultrasounds (every 2-4 weeks vs every 4 weeks). Higher caloric needs (about 600 extra calories/day vs 300). More weight gain (37-54 lbs for healthy BMI vs 25-35 lbs). Higher risk of: gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, preterm labor, and C-section. Average twin delivery: 36 weeks (vs 39 for singletons). Expect more discomfort earlier — everything is amplified with two.

Preparing for Two

Double the gear isn't always necessary: you need 2 car seats and 2 cribs, but can share a changing table, baby monitor, and many other items. Consider a twin-specific stroller (tandem or side-by-side). Stock extra supplies: double the diapers, double the formula if bottle-feeding. Build a support network — caring for twins is physically demanding and sleep deprivation is intense. Accept all help offered.
