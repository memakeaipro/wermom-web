10 Best Tummy Time Activities by Age (0-6 Months)

Parenting GuideBy Wermom Medical TeamUpdated March 20268 min read
Quick Answer: Tummy time builds the neck, shoulder, arm, and core strength your baby needs for every major motor milestone. These age-specific activities make it fun instead of frustrating.

The Complete List

  1. 1. Chest-to-chest tummy time (0-4 weeks) — lay baby on your chest while you recline; your heartbeat is soothing and face is motivating
  2. 2. Football hold (0-8 weeks) — carry baby face-down along your forearm for passive tummy time while you walk around
  3. 3. Rolled towel under chest (4-8 weeks) — a small rolled towel under baby's chest takes some weight off arms, making it easier
  4. 4. High-contrast cards at eye level (4-12 weeks) — place black-and-white images 8-12 inches from baby's face to encourage head lifting
  5. 5. Mirror play (8-16 weeks) — unbreakable mirror at floor level; babies are fascinated by faces and will lift head to look
  6. 6. Toy just out of reach (12-20 weeks) — place a favorite toy slightly ahead to encourage reaching and weight shifting
  7. 7. Airplane rides (12-20 weeks) — hold baby horizontal with hands supporting chest and tummy; gentle movement builds core strength
  8. 8. Boppy/nursing pillow support (12-24 weeks) — drape baby over a nursing pillow for supported tummy time with better visual range
  9. 9. Water mat (16-24 weeks) — inflatable water mat provides visual stimulation and encourages reaching/pushing movements
  10. 10. Tummy time on parent's lap (any age) — lay baby across your thighs and gently pat/rub back; good for babies who resist floor time

Why This Matters

These aren't just random tips — they're drawn from pediatric research, nurse expertise, and thousands of real parent experiences. Each item addresses a specific need that new parents commonly face. Bookmark this list and come back to it as your baby grows.
