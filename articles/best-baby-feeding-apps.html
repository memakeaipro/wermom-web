Best Baby Feeding & Nutrition Apps for New Parents (2026)

Whether you're breastfeeding, formula feeding, combination feeding, or introducing solids, tracking what your baby eats is important. The right app can help you understand feeding patterns, ensure adequate nutrition, and provide guidance as your baby's feeding needs evolve.

#1: Wermom — Best for Comprehensive Nutrition Planning

Wermom

Price: $24.99/month | Rating: 4.9/5

Wermom provides personalized nutrition planning that adapts to your baby's age, current feeding method, and any family allergies or preferences. Unlike generic meal plans, Wermom personalizes based on your specific situation.

Feeding features: Feeding frequency and volume recommendations, breast vs. formula tracking, solids introduction guidance aligned with developmental readiness, personalized meal plans by age/trimester, allergy-aware meal planning, nutritional adequacy tracking, postpartum nutrition for mother.

Why it's best for nutrition: Integrates feeding data with overall health assessment. If your baby's growth is tracking at the 60th percentile, nutrition recommendations account for that individual trajectory, not generic "babies need X oz."

#2: Milk Man — Best for Breastfeeding Tracking

Milk Man (Exclusively Breastfeeding)

Price: Free with premium | Rating: 4.4/5

Specialized for breastfeeding mothers tracking feeding duration, frequency, and supply patterns.

Features: Feeding duration per breast, output indicators, supply tracking, medication compatibility checker, lactation consultant directory.

Best for: Exclusively breastfeeding mothers who want detailed breastfeeding-specific tracking.

#3: Total Baby — Combination Feeding Focus

Total Baby

Price: Free with premium | Rating: 4.2/5

Designed to handle combination feeding (breast and formula) tracking elegantly, which many apps struggle with.

Features: Side-by-side breast/formula tracking, flexible logging, output tracking, growth charts, milestone tracking.

Best for: Parents combining breast and formula feeding who need an app that handles both equally.

#4: BabyCenter — General Feeding Info

BabyCenter

Price: Free | Rating: 4.2/5

Includes feeding tracking and extensive articles about feeding methods.

Features: Feeding logs, basic feeding guidance, community discussion, articles about solids introduction.

Best for: Free general feeding information and community support.

#5: SeekHeals — Professional Lactation Support

SeekHeals

Price: Consultation-based | Rating: 4.6/5

Connects you with professional lactation consultants for personalized guidance.

Features: LC directory, remote consultations, problem-specific guidance, personalized recommendations.

Best for: Breastfeeding parents needing professional support beyond app guidance.
