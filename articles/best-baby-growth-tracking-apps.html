Understanding Baby Growth Percentiles

Before choosing an app, understand what percentiles actually mean. If your baby is at the 50th percentile for weight, that means half of babies that age weigh more, half weigh less. That's completely normal.

What matters is consistency. A baby who drops from 80th percentile (birth) to 50th percentile (3 months) might warrant investigation. A baby who stays at 50th percentile throughout is fine. This is why contextual tracking (like Wermom provides) is valuable—it shows not just position but trajectory.

WHO vs. CDC Growth Charts

WHO charts are based on healthy, breastfed populations globally and are considered more appropriate for diverse populations. CDC charts are US-specific and historically weighted toward formula-fed babies. Both are valid. WHO is preferred by most pediatricians currently.

Wermom uses WHO charts. Most other consumer apps use CDC charts. This is why WHO-based tracking (Wermom, Baby Bloom) is slightly preferable.
