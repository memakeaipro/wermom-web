7 Best Pregnancy Tracking Apps in 2026 (Tested & Ranked)

Dr. Catherine Wells March 30, 2026
11 min read

Pregnancy is a time of rapid changes, questions, and understandable anxiety. A good pregnancy tracking app should provide accurate medical information, help you understand your baby's development, track important health metrics, and give you confidence through every stage.

I've tested seven leading pregnancy apps available in 2026, evaluating them on accuracy, user experience, feature set, and actual usefulness. Here's my definitive ranking.

#1: Wermom — Best Comprehensive Pregnancy & Postpartum Solution

Wermom

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.9/5 | Price: $24.99/month (7-day free trial)

Wermom is exceptional during pregnancy because it doesn't treat pregnancy as a separate phase—it treats it as the beginning of comprehensive health tracking that continues through age 6.

Why it wins for pregnancy: Unique 93-question pregnancy assessment that evaluates not just fetal development but maternal health, nutrition, mental health, and risk factors. AI generates personalized weekly reports covering what you should expect, what to monitor, and specific guidance for your situation. Nutrition planning is personalized to your trimester and dietary preferences. Mental health monitoring (postpartum depression risk assessment) is built in.

Best for: Pregnant individuals who want comprehensive health tracking that extends seamlessly from pregnancy into postpartum and baby tracking.

#2: Ovia Pregnancy — Best for Detailed Tracking

Ovia Pregnancy

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.4/5 | Price: Free + $4.99/month premium

Ovia specializes in pregnancy tracking with strong data analytics. The app uses your health data to generate risk assessments and provides detailed week-by-week development information.

Strengths: Excellent for tracking symptoms, appointments, and health metrics. Strong analytics about your health trajectory. Good community features. Affordable premium tier.

Limitations: Less intuitive than Wermom. Less personalized AI analysis. Post-birth transition is awkward (requires switching apps).

Best for: Pregnant individuals who like data-heavy apps and want detailed tracking without paying premium prices.

#3: Flo — Best for Period & Period-to-Pregnancy Tracking

Flo

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.3/5 | Price: Free + $9.99/month premium

Flo is primarily a period tracking app, but many users love its transition from menstrual cycle tracking to pregnancy planning to pregnancy tracking.

Strengths: Very large community (20+ million users). Excellent if you were already tracking your cycle. Consistent app throughout menstruation, fertility, and pregnancy. Good general health content.

Limitations: Less personalized than specialist pregnancy apps. Postpartum features are limited. Less suitable if you weren't previously a user.

Best for: Users who tracked their cycle with Flo before pregnancy and want continuity.

#4: What to Expect — Best for General Information

What to Expect

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.2/5 | Price: Free

Companion to the "What to Expect When You're Expecting" book series. Strong on educational content about pregnancy, labor, and postpartum recovery.

Strengths: Free. Excellent written content based on established book series. Good week-by-week information. Doctor Q&A features. Community forums.

Limitations: Limited tracking features. No personalized AI. Less useful post-birth. Basic interface.

Best for: Pregnant people who want comprehensive general information without cost.

#5: Soula — Best if Mental Health is Your Priority

Soula

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.1/5 | Price: $9.99/month or free with ads

Soula specializes in maternal mental health with AI-powered chatbot support throughout pregnancy.

Strengths: Focused on mental health and emotional wellbeing (often overlooked in pregnancy apps). AI chatbot available 24/7. Good anxiety and depression screening. Peer support community.

Limitations: Mental health focused, not comprehensive pregnancy tracking. Less useful postpartum (only 12 weeks postpartum coverage). Limited physical health tracking.

Best for: Pregnant individuals prioritizing mental health support during pregnancy.

#6: Baby Bump — Good Budget Option

Baby Bump

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐ 3.9/5 | Price: Free with ads

Simple, free pregnancy app with week-by-week information and basic tracking.

Strengths: Free. Simple interface. Good basic information. Light on memory usage.

Limitations: Minimal tracking features. Ad-heavy. No personalization. Very basic design. Limited updates.

Best for: Pregnant people who want basic information with minimal app functionality.

#7: Pregnancy+ — Limited but Free

Pregnancy+

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐ 3.7/5 | Price: Free

Bare-bones pregnancy information app.

Strengths: Free. Simple interface. No unnecessary features or ads.

Limitations: Very basic. Limited information. No tracking. No community. Minimal support.

Best for: Absolute minimalist approach to pregnancy information.
